What Was Built — In 5 Steps

Phases
1
Brand Alignment

Replaced the default purple theme with Bejundas's navy + blue palette. Added the "B" monogram as the sidebar logo and browser favicon. Now the admin and the public website feel like the same brand world.

2
Custom Dashboard

Built a real overview screen instead of a database list. Shows total leads, new leads this week, unread leads (highlighted in red), and a colour-coded chart of leads by business vertical — all the moment you log in.

3
Organized Sidebar

Grouped every section into clear categories: Dashboard, Website Content, Leads, Site Settings, Administration. Added an icon to each item, plus a live red badge on "All Leads" that shows how many are still awaiting follow-up.

4
Settings Split Into 7 Focused Sections

Site Settings used to be one giant form with 30+ fields stacked together. Now it's seven clean sections — Identity & Branding, Hero, About, Mission & Vision, CTA, Contact, Social Media — each opening to just the fields that matter for that part of the site.

5
Polish & Convenience

Added colour-coded status pills (red NEW, green NOTIFIED, amber DRAFT, etc.), thumbnail previews for team photos and news covers, vertical brand colour swatches, and a "mark multiple leads as notified" batch button.

What You Can Do Faster Now

Workflow Wins
→
See unread leads instantly The moment you log in, the red badge in the sidebar tells you exactly how many leads need your follow-up. No more scrolling through tables.
→
Update only the part of the site you care about Want to change the home page hero text? Click "Hero Section". Just the right form opens — no scrolling past Address, Mission, Social, etc.
→
Mark multiple leads as notified at once After replying to a batch of enquiries, tick the boxes and use the "Mark as notified" action. Done in one click instead of opening each lead.
→
Spot a published vs draft article at a glance News articles show a green LIVE pill or amber DRAFT pill in the list, plus a thumbnail of the cover image. No need to open each one.
→
Verify each business vertical's brand colours The Vertical Placeholders list shows two coloured dots per row (primary + accent). One look confirms each Coming Soon page is themed correctly.
